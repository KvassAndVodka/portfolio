# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users
Tech recruiters, hiring managers, and developers looking to evaluate technical skills, projects, and writing. Visitors typically view pages on desktop and mobile browsers, often with low patience and high expectations of clarity.

## Product Purpose
A self-hosted personal portfolio and blog platform to showcase engineering projects and publish technical writing. Success is defined by memorable visual presentation, clear navigation to projects, and high reading engagement on blog posts.

## Positioning
A highly polished, self-hosted developer portfolio demonstrating production-grade full-stack capability through expressive interaction and concrete proof.

## Conversion & proof
- Primary and secondary CTA: View Github repository projects, Read technical blog posts
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: A serious engineer who makes dependable systems feel alive.
- Belief ladder:
  1. This engineer writes clean, well-structured, production-grade code.
  2. They possess strong full-stack skills (React, Next.js, Postgres, Docker).
  3. They care about design quality, accessibility, and performance details.
- Proof on hand: Open-source project repositories, live deployable demos, and technical articles.

## Brand Personality
Energetic, technical, assured. The emotional goal is curious engineering confidence with enough restraint to keep the work credible.

## Anti-references
Generic SaaS dashboard aesthetics, blue-to-purple background gradients, over-nested cards, and excessive rounding (`32px+` borders).

## Design Principles
- **Expressive Systems:** Motion and interaction should make the underlying engineering mindset visible.
- **Typographic Clarity:** Clear type scale using high-contrast colors (body contrast ≥ 4.5:1).
- **Intentional Motion:** Give each major moment a distinct behavior while keeping reading and navigation immediate.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Full keyboard accessibility, contrast complying with WCAG 2.1 AA standards, and respect for `prefers-reduced-motion` settings.
